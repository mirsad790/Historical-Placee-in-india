Top Historical Places in India

Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal in Agra is an iconic symbol of love, built by Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal.

Red Fort

The Red Fort in Delhi is a UNESCO World Heritage site, representing the pinnacle of Mughal architecture.

Qutub Minar

Qutub Minar in Delhi is the tallest brick minaret in the world, reflecting the brilliance of Indo-Islamic architecture.

Hampi

Hampi in Karnataka is a UNESCO World Heritage site known for its ruins and ancient temples from the Vijayanagara Empire.

Amer Fort

Amer Fort in Jaipur is a magnificent fort blending Hindu and Mughal architectural styles, built in the 16th century.

Konark Sun Temple

The Konark Sun Temple in Odisha is a 13th-century temple dedicated to the Sun God and is a UNESCO World Heritage site.

Gateway of India

The Gateway of India in Mumbai was built to commemorate the visit of King George V and Queen Mary to India in 1911.

Khajuraho Temples

The Khajuraho Temples in Madhya Pradesh are known for their stunning carvings and are a UNESCO World Heritage site.

Hazarduari Palaces

Hazarduari is an iconic palace, located in Murshidabad, the most popular tourist spot in Murshidabad.
